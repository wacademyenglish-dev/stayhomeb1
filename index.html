Syntax Framework

When we talk about habits and preferences, the second verb's format changes depending on the first verb.

Verb + Gerund (ing)

Used for enjoyment, avoidance, or processes.

  • I enjoy staying home.
  • They avoid going out.
  • She finishes working late.
enjoy avoid finish mind

Verb + Infinitive (to)

Used for needs, plans, or future intentions.

  • I need to recharge.
  • We decided to stay.
  • He wants to read.
need want decide plan

Advanced Protocols

Warning: Some verbs completely change their meaning depending on what follows.

Stop + Gerund

To quit an action entirely.

"I stopped going to clubs."

(I don't go to clubs anymore. It's over.)

Stop + Infinitive

To interrupt what you are doing in order to do something else.

"I stopped to drink water."

(I was walking, I paused, and then I drank water.)

The Extrovert Ideal

In her book Quiet, Susan Cain argues that modern Western culture misunderstands and undervalues the traits of introverted people. Society is dominated by the "Extrovert Ideal"—the belief that the ideal self is gregarious, alpha, and comfortable in the spotlight.

As a result, schools and workplaces are designed for extroverts, with open-plan offices and constant group work. This forces introverts to act like extroverts, which is exhausting and ignores the deep creativity that comes from solitude.
